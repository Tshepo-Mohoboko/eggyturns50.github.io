Our Story

1971 - 1981

 
In the first decade, we saw the Lord bringing forth the emergence of a Queen, Eggy Nkidi Dinah Mohoboko. It was on a glorious Wednesday afternoon when this bundle of Joy announced her presence. She was born to Mr Reuben and Mrs Paulina Gaborone, being the fourth and last daughter. Eggy as affectionately known was welcomed by three of her siblings, Rose, Gladys and Tshidi. As you’d recall that during those dark days of segregation and separate development, it was unheard of to have people of colour to be in the vicinity of areas demarcated for other races of lesser melanin at night. However, as the Lord would have it, I was graced by God’s favour to be rushed to a white-only designated hospital in the middle of the night, at 14 days old, having being terrorized by a bout of acute bronchitis. The Lord had already put for me a German Doctor—MaVenter, to treat and nurse me back to life when most people would have given up hope. At that tender age, I received my first injection and was given a recommended nutritional diet to follow in dealing with my delicate condition. Hmm, so much God’s favour was just an indication of the unfolding of many such incidences of favour. 50 years down the line, here I stand and proclaim victory in his name, Victory in the blood of the Lamb. Ebenezer, Nkosi Jesu, u ndi gcinile, kwaze kwa la.


1981 - 1991



 
The second decade came with much trepidation and caution as I traversed into unknown teen territory. During the period in question I was very much a resident of two provinces as we constantly moved from one place to the other. The stresses of not fully belonging neither here nor there dented my image as I could not consistently keep a set of friends because of the constant movements. I always felt like a newcomer in both places. The International year of the youth brought with it an awareness of and interest in the struggle, taking in the commotion caused by prevalent riots. I witness the full wrath of police brutality as well as masses courage to beat the system. All of that happened through the high-school years, which meant for me the focus had to be on the books only – nothing else. One remarkable life-changing moment of the decade for me was my acceptance of Jesus Christ as my personal Lord and Saviour in 1985—a decision I am very grateful for. This decision set me on an upward trajectory that grounded me on a set of Christian principles and value-sets that shaped my character and distinguished me from other peers with different lifestyles. I look back in retrospect, and thank God for having found me then. It was Grace that taught my heart to fear, and grace will definitely lead me home. Ebenezer Nkosi yam—wa hamba nam kwaze kwa la.


1991 - 2001

 
In the third decade things got a bit more serious, with the focus turning to tertiary studies and a journey of rigorous training and development – not without my share of challenges. I recall my anxieties about going to University having completed my matriculation from a township school without basic amenities like laboratory or library. At the time I couldn’t even string along a coherent sentence in proper English, but hey, all of that is behind me now. That shy and almost timid young girl can take on any stage now without any hesitation. We couldn’t afford much then, and the thought of being away from home without bursaries and enough sets of jeans looked daunting at the time. Jehovah Jireh being who he is, saw me through the entire journey and I lacked nothing. Notwithstanding the hills and valleys, the same grace showed up step by step – never once was I made to feel inferior as I traversed the journey. The love and support from family made me surefooted and the journey was manageable! Little did I know that God had the biggest surprise for me – a treasure of my life, packaged as my very own Prince Charming, Mr Liphapang John Mohoboko came by and swept me off my feet, defocusing my attention to a whole new world, never travelled before. He took me to the Altar and made out of me an incredible mother of three, Tshepo, Tlotliso and Letlotlo – by God’s amazing grace, we’re 27 years in the journey, and it still feels good. So many lessons learnt, so many memories created, and if I were to do it all over again, I wouldn’t change a thing. Through it all, we’ve learnt to trust in Jesus, and we’ve learnt to trust in God. Ebenezer, Nkosi Jesu – you deserve the praise!


2001 - 2011

 
In the fourth decade the focus of my life was on the occupational sphere. Within the Public service at different levels of execution, I was entrusted with positions of responsibility. As a young, black female, I had to work harder and smarter than my older male counterparts and what an uphill battle it was. Since this girl is empowered by Jesus Christ I took it all in good stride, displaying qualities such as commitment, dedication, tenacity and resilience throughout the journey. Promotions were hard to come by as the system of the world oftentimes demands what some of us cannot offer. Nonetheless, at the appointed time, The Lord Himself, caused it to happen. The lesson learnt throughout this journey is that unto whom much is given, much is required. With each level came added expectations that drove me to forge ahead without cringing because I can do all things through Christ that gives me strength. I remained determined to discharge all my responsibilities as unto the Lord, knowing that my reward is great in heaven. I have totally been dependant on God’s guidance, wisdom and protection in all these years and indeed, not once did He ever leave nor forsake me. Ebenezer Nkosi Jesu, Umusa wakho siwubonile!!!!


2011 - Present

 
The fifth decade came through and as they say life begins at 40, so the journey of self-discovery and self-awareness commenced. Truth be told, some of us started much later than others, and the journey of being content with who I am was quiet interesting. It was in this stage of life that I begin to truthfully discover what makes me happy for me, as all the time prior was about being the best for everyone else. I had to be a good daughter, scholar, student, employee, wife, mother, cousin, church-member – so it was about everyone else but me. I had to go and rediscover where I last lost Eggy, the girl with her own dreams, and the discovery was much more fulfilling. The self-discovery journey empowered me to hone my skills of self-awareness and self-mastery in such a manner that I am now better able to handle complex situations with much calm and ease, yet produce amazing results. The awareness of my environment, my abilities and my short-comings helped me to be at ease with this Masterpiece you see today with a full understanding that I am God’s workmanship – an apple of His eye. I am content with what I see in the mirror and other people’s opinions about me are just that – their opinions. What you see today as you look at me is a product of refinement, and the work of the Blood. Ebenezer Nkosi Jesu – Grace upon grace…
